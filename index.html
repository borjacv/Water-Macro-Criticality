How the tiers are estimated

The tiers are a structured qualitative synthesis, not a statistically estimated index. Each country is scored 0–3 on four dimensions of water macro-criticality, and the equally-weighted average gives a 0–3 composite that sets the tier.

  • Scarcity (S) — baseline physical water stress (WRI Aqueduct 4.0).
  • Economic exposure (E) — reliance on irrigated agriculture, hydropower, tourism, or sheer size of exposed GDP.
  • Fiscal vulnerability (F) — drought-to-budget transmission, subsidies, underfunding, thin debt space.
  • External exposure (X) — reliance on imported virtual water / food / energy and transboundary rivers.

Composite = average of the four scores (0–3). Tiers: ≥ 2.25 = Tier 1 · 1.50–2.24 = Tier 2 · 1.00–1.49 = Tier 3.

Notes: fiscal capacity is treated as a genuine offset, so the physically extreme Gulf states sit in Tier 2 rather than Tier 1; India ranks high partly on absolute scale. A companion Excel workbook lists every country’s four sub-scores with adjustable weights, so the ranking is fully reproducible and auditable.
